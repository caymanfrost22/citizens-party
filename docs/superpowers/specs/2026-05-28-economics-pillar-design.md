## 2. Signature Principle (NEW)

### Captivity = Regulation. Choice = Market.

> If a business restricts the customer's ability to self-supply, substitute, or walk away, government caps markup at 5% or breaks the captivity. If customers can freely choose, market sets the price.

**Applies cross-pillar:**

| Captive context | Free context |
|-----------------|--------------|
| Disney parks (no outside food) → 5% markup cap OR allow outside food | Open restaurant — price freely |
| Apple App Store (no sideloading) → 5% cap OR allow sideloading | Web app — price freely |
| Sports stadium concessions (no outside food) → 5% cap OR allow outside food | Concert at park — price freely |
| Hospital cafeteria (locked-in patient) → 5% cap | Restaurant near hospital — price freely |
| Prison commissary (literal captive) → 5% cap | Outside store — price freely |
| Ticketmaster (sole vendor for venue) → 5% cap OR allow secondary sale | Open ticket market — price freely |
| PBMs (employer/insurer picks for you, opaque) → break vertical integration | Direct retail pharmacy — market |
| Medicare/Medicaid drug captive market → negotiate prices | Self-pay OTC — market |

**Why it matters:** Reconciles our libertarian instinct (no structural breakups of Amazon, Meta, etc.) with our anti-monopoly instinct (force PBM divestiture, cap App Store fees). The deciding question is always: *Did the consumer choose this relationship freely?*

## 3. Sub-Issue Positions

### 3.1 Debt + Federal Spending

**Target:** Debt-to-GDP under 50% in 20–30 years (currently 127%). Matches historical achievements: UK post-WWII, Canada 1990s, NZ, Sweden.

**Social Security Reform:**
- **Retirement age: 59½** (matches 401(k) penalty-free withdrawal age). Clean — same age both retirement systems work.
- **Payroll tax tiering:** 6.2% on wages up to $200K. **2.5% on wages above $200K, uncapped.** CEO making $20M pays into SS on every dollar, just at a lower rate above the threshold.
- **Benefit cap:** Max SS payout = $200K/yr (current max ~$61K at age 70). Generous ceiling for upper earners, finite.
- **Trust fund investment:** Move from Treasury-only to **sovereign wealth fund** (Canada CPPIB / Norway model). Independent professional management, diversified global portfolio (equities, bonds, infrastructure, real assets), politically firewalled from Congress raiding it. Historical CPPIB returns ~10% annual.
- **Drug price negotiation:** Expand Medicare negotiation list aggressively. Stacks with healthcare sub-issue.
- **Honest math:** Use accrual accounting. Stop counting trust fund balances as both an asset and a liability of the same government.

**Defense:**
- Maintain ~$850B baseline (not a cut for cuts' sake).
- **Audit accountability:** Pentagon failed audit every year 2018–present. Mandatory annual audit. **Failure → automatic 5% budget cut next FY + replace SecDef + service chiefs.** Congress can waive cut by majority vote if failure is justified by force-majeure (declared disaster, war).
- Peace through strength + reshored defense manufacturing + AI/tech modernization (cross-link Trade sub-issue).
- Force structure flex pending Foreign Policy pillar (separate pillar, future work).

**Enforcement mechanisms (all four, stacked):**
- **Balanced Budget Amendment** — except in declared war or recession.
- **PAYGO with teeth** — new spending offset by cuts or revenue. No emergency loopholes for non-emergencies.
- **Sunset clause on every law AND every agency** — 10-year clock. Existing laws phased in over first 10 yrs. Forces continuous review. Massive bureaucracy-shrink mechanism.
- **Debt-to-GDP target enforced by automatic cuts** — sequestration-style but actually enforced if trajectory misses.

**Discretionary cuts:** **Performance audits + funding tied to outcomes.** Every program has measurable metrics. Miss targets 3 years running → funding cut. Real accountability for results, not effort.

**Dem position (for /issues table):** Tax the rich to spend more on programs. Don't touch entitlements. Defend defense baseline.
**Rep position:** Cut welfare and discretionary programs. Don't raise taxes. Protect Pentagon.
**Citizens Party:** Honest math + invest SS like Canada + lower retirement age + uncap payroll above $200K at 2.5% + cap benefits at $200K + Pentagon audit teeth + sunset everything 10-yr + BBA + PAYGO + performance funding.

### 3.2 Healthcare Costs

**(Cost side only. Healthcare access — single-payer debate, Medicare-for-All — is a separate Social Pillar sub-issue.)**

**Drug pricing:**
- **International reference pricing** for ALL drugs sold in US. Price capped at median of 6 peer nations (UK, Germany, France, Canada, Australia, Japan). Applies to private market too, not just Medicare.
- **Legalize drug imports** from Canada + EU (currently blocked by FDA).

**Pharmacy Benefit Managers (PBMs):**
- **Ban manufacturer rebates.** Pass-through pricing only — all discounts go to patient at the counter. PBM fee is flat per-prescription.
- **Force vertical divestiture.** Insurer-owns-PBM-owns-pharmacy (UnitedHealth/Optum, CVS/Aetna/Caremark) is the rent-extraction engine. Antitrust enforcement: forced separation.
- Justifies under Captivity = Regulation principle: employer/insurer picks PBM for you, you can't shop.

**Hospital + provider transparency:**
- **Real-time public pricing API** — every hospital, insurer, provider publishes actual negotiated rates in machine-readable format. (Already required by 2021 rule, near-universally ignored.) **Non-compliance penalty: 5% Medicare reimbursement cut + public listing.**
- **Binding upfront price quotes** — before any non-emergency procedure, patient gets a written binding quote. No surprise billing.
- **Site-neutral payments** — Medicare pays the same rate for the same procedure regardless of facility type. Kills hospital-outpatient-vs-clinic arbitrage.

**Insurance admin overhead:**
- **Single national claims standard** — one standardized claims format across all insurers. Eliminates billions in administrative friction.
- **Portable patient records** — federal right to download your full medical record in one standard format. Your data, not theirs.

**Hospital monopoly / consolidation:** Trust transparency + site-neutral payments to self-correct. Don't directly attack consolidation (70% of US hospital markets are concentrated). If transparency + site-neutral kills the consolidation arbitrage, markets de-concentrate naturally.

**Patient-side tools:**
- **Universal HSA from birth** — every American has an HSA. Federal seed $500/yr for kids, higher match for low-income. Lifetime tax-advantaged health account.
- **Direct Primary Care (DPC) subscriptions** ($50–100/mo flat for unlimited PCP visits) qualify as HSA expense + tax-deductible.
- **Expanded HSA contribution limits** — well above current $4,150 individual / $8,300 family caps.
- Net effect: insurance trends toward catastrophic-only; routine care is direct patient-provider, no middleman.

**Dem position:** Expand ACA, public option, limited drug negotiation (10 drugs).
**Rep position:** HSAs only, market competition, repeal ACA, less regulation.
**Citizens Party:** International reference pricing + drug imports + PBM rebate ban + PBM divestiture + real-time public pricing + binding quotes + site-neutral + unified claims + portable records + Universal HSA from birth + DPC subscriptions.

### 3.3 Housing Affordability

**Investor concentration:**
- **Domestic institutions:** 4% annual surtax on assessed value of holdings >50 single-family units. No hard cap (lets market work), but hoarding becomes uneconomical. BlackRock, Blackstone, Invitation Homes, etc.
- **Foreign corporations:** Banned from buying US residential.
- **Foreign individuals legally resident in US:** Treated same as US families — can own up to 10 properties. Above 10 = "holding" status, subject to surtax.
- **Holding threshold:** 10 properties for any owner (individual or entity, US or foreign-resident). 10+ = holding = surtax.

**Zoning:** Federal incentives, not preemption.
- HUD + DOT federal dollars contingent on YIMBY reforms: legalize duplex/triplex/ADU by-right on SF lots, eliminate parking minimums, max 6-month permit review.
- Carrot, not stick. Local control retained.

**Federal supply unlock:**
- **Controlled federal land release.** Feds own 28% of US land (640M acres). Release tracts near growing metros — but in controlled, accessible way to prevent large companies from gobbling and rent-seeking. Tracts allocated via lottery or first-come for builders meeting size + affordability constraints.
- **Fannie/Freddie reform** — preferred mortgage rates (1pt below market) for owner-occupied first-time buyers. De-prioritize investor mortgages.
- **Phase out Mortgage Interest Deduction** over 5 years. MID is regressive (~$600K-plus benefit goes to top 20%). Redirect savings into first-time-buyer Housing Savings Account.
- **Cap REIT residential depreciation** — REITs deduct "phantom" depreciation on rental property, making rental more profitable than owning. Cap or eliminate for residential REITs.

**First-time buyer support:**
- **Housing Savings Account (HSA-style)** — tax-deferred savings specifically for first home down payment. Federal 1:1 match up to $5K/yr for low/middle income. Patient-aligned, market-aligned — no subsidy to seller, no price-capitalization effect.

**Dem position:** Down payment subsidies ($25K first-time buyer), LIHTC expansion, rent regulation.
**Rep position:** Deregulation, opportunity zones, federal hands-off.
**Citizens Party:** Domestic investor surtax + foreign corp ban + zoning carrots + controlled federal land release + Fannie/Freddie FTB priority + MID phase-out + REIT depreciation cap + Housing Savings Account.

### 3.4 Inflation / Cost of Living

Many anti-inflation levers are already pulled by sibling sub-issues (housing supply, healthcare cost, debt/spending). This sub-issue covers **Fed reform**, **money supply discipline**, **energy abundance**, and **household resilience**.

**Federal Reserve reform:**
- **Single mandate: price stability only.** Strip the "max employment" half of dual mandate (1977 Humphrey-Hawkins). One job: 2% inflation. Employment is a fiscal/regulatory outcome, not monetary policy job. ECB and Bank of England model.
- Pairs with M2 cap below: no excuses about jobs when prices spiral.

**Money supply discipline:**
- **Hard cap on M2 growth.** Fed cannot grow money supply faster than nominal GDP + 1%. Prevents COVID-era money printing (M2 grew 40% in 2 years 2020–22, then inflation spiked 25% cumulative through 2025).

**Energy abundance:**
- **All-of-the-above leaning nuclear.** SMRs (Small Modular Reactors), restart shutdown plants, allow advanced reactor designs. Datacenter campuses should host on-site nuclear (their power demand justifies dedicated generation).
- All other sources: oil, gas, geothermal, renewables, grid modernization.
- **12-month federal permitting cap on any energy project.** Current process: 4–7 years.
- Cross-link: full energy policy lives in Environment pillar (future).

**Cost-of-living shock response:**
- **Skip emergency response. Fix systemic causes.**
- No CPI auto-indexed safety net beyond what exists. No emergency tax-pause mechanisms. No price-gouging laws.
- Rationale: emergency mechanisms encourage more inflationary spending and political abuse. Fix the Fed + supply + money + spending and the shocks stop happening.

**Dem position:** Blame supply chains + corporate greed, defend Fed independence, expand safety net.
**Rep position:** Blame Biden spending, cut taxes, pro-energy.
**Citizens Party:** Fed single mandate + M2 ≤ NGDP+1% hard cap + all-of-the-above-leaning-nuclear energy + 12-mo permit cap + no emergency response (fix systemic causes).

### 3.5 Big Tech / Antitrust

**Structural breakups: NO.**
- Meta can own WhatsApp + Instagram. Amazon can sell its own products on Amazon. Apple can pre-install Apple apps. Google can rank Google services. **Free market for chosen relationships.** Users freely chose these platforms. They can leave. They can use competitors.
- Citizens Party stance: platforms must follow universal laws (speech, contracts, fraud), but government does not dictate corporate structure.

**Markup cap rule (under signature principle):**
- **If a business restricts the customer's ability to self-supply, markup capped at 5%.**
- **If the business allows self-supply or alternatives, markup is free.**
- It's an XOR: cap-or-choice, never both.
- App Store: cap 5% on in-app purchases, OR allow third-party stores + sideloading. Apple picks.
- Disney parks: cap markup on park food/drink at 5%, OR allow guests to bring outside food. Disney picks.
- Stadiums, theaters, Ticketmaster, prison commissary, hospital cafeterias: same.

**Section 230 — size-based tiering + algorithmic amplification liability:**
- **Small platforms (<10M US users):** Full 230 protection. Preserves innovation, indie services, new entrants.
- **Mega-platforms (>100M US users):** Liable for content their algorithm actively amplifies. Posts users discover organically still 230-shielded. Posts the algorithm pushed to millions → platform is responsible.
- **Mandatory chronological / no-algorithm feed option** on mega-platforms. User chooses to opt INTO algorithmic feed. Default is chronological. Restores user control.

**Data ownership + privacy:**
- **Opt-in default for all data collection** (GDPR-style). No collection without explicit user opt-in. Pre-checked boxes illegal. Major shift from current opt-out world.

**AI:** Deferred to dedicated future sub-issue. Touch only edges in Big Tech (e.g. AI antitrust if it arises). Big Tech scope stays tight.

**Dem position:** Privacy laws, narrow 230 for misinformation, antitrust enforcement on monopolies.
**Rep position:** Narrow 230 for censorship, antitrust on perceived bias.
**Citizens Party:** No structural breakups + markup cap XOR self-supply + 230 size-tiering + algorithmic-amplification liability + chronological feed opt-out + opt-in privacy default.

### 3.6 Trade + Tariffs

**Tariff philosophy: revenue + fairness, NOT universal baseline.**

Tariff rates depend on TWO axes:

1. **Country relationship:**
   - **Friend countries** (those whose trade policies actively help USA — reciprocal access, IP respect, no dumping, security alliance): Zero tariffs on most goods. Lower tariffs on strategic goods.
   - **Reciprocal countries** (mixed): Match what they charge us. If they charge 20% on US autos, we charge 20% on theirs.
   - **Adversarial countries:** Higher tariffs across the board, especially strategic.

2. **Good category:**
   - **Strategic industries** (defense, semiconductors, pharma APIs, rare earths, energy, food staples): Higher tariffs across all countries. Lower for friends, higher for others.
   - **Non-strategic goods:** Country-relationship-based.

No universal 10% baseline. No flat global tariff that raises consumer prices on every import. Tariffs are revenue + leverage, not isolationism.

**China specifically:**
- **Targeted bans only.** Ban Chinese ownership of US critical infrastructure (farmland near bases, ports, telecom, data centers near military installations). Ban sensitive tech imports (military-applicable). Force divestiture of mass-surveillance apps (TikTok-class) by Chinese owners.
- **Do not** decouple all trade, do not impose 60% tariff wall on everything. Lets non-strategic trade continue, captures strategic vulnerabilities.

**Country of origin:**
- **55% value-added rule.** A product's country of origin is where >55% of value is added. Closes the transhipment loophole (Chinese goods lightly processed in Mexico/Vietnam and entering as "Mexican" to dodge China tariffs).
- Customs audits with penalty teeth.

**Strategic supply chain:**
- **Mandatory 180-day domestic stockpiles** of: pharma APIs, semiconductors, rare earths, ammunition, fertilizer, baby formula. Govt buys + rotates inventory, Strategic Petroleum Reserve model expanded.
- **Reshoring tax credit** (25% of capex) for any company reshoring strategic manufacturing. **Hard sunset at 10 years.** No permanent industrial policy.
- **Buy American expansion**: federal procurement 100% domestic for strategic goods + 75% for everything else (DOD, VA, GSA, etc.). Govt as anchor customer.
- **12-month permitting fast-track** for strategic manufacturing plants (same rule as energy — same rule, applied twice).

**Dem position:** Traditional free trade, targeted Buy American, limited tariffs.
**Rep position:** Broad tariffs as revenue + reshoring tool, China decouple, 10% universal + 60% China.
**Citizens Party:** Strategic + reciprocal tariffs (NOT universal) + China targeted bans + 55% value-added origin + 180-day strategic stockpiles + reshoring credit (10-yr sunset) + Buy American 100%/75% + 12-mo permitting fast-track.

### 3.7 Wages / Jobs / Labor

**Minimum wage — tiered by age + hours:**
- **Under 18 OR working <25 hrs/wk:** ~$10/hr floor. CPI-indexed.
- **18+ AND 25+ hrs/wk:** ~$15/hr floor. CPI + regional cost-of-living adjustment (BLS publishes — high-cost metros adjust up, rural stays at floor).
- Rationale: mom-and-pop shops can hire high-school kids and part-timers without union-grade wage cost. Career roles get a true living wage. Federal floor in both tiers — states can go higher.
- Both tiers auto-index annually. No more 16-year freezes.

**Gig workers (Uber, DoorDash, Instacart):**
- **3rd federal classification: "Independent Worker"** — keeps flexibility (workers can choose hours, multiple platforms), but platforms must pay into **portable benefits** per hour worked (health, retirement, disability). Account owned by worker, moves across platforms.
- Sidesteps binary employee-vs-contractor fight. Both Dem (reclassify all as employees) and Rep (status quo contractor) positions force false choice.

**Non-competes:**
- **Banned for any worker earning under $150K/yr.** Includes fast-food, retail, janitorial, most knowledge work. Unenforceable.
- **Above $150K:** Allowed, but max 12 months, AND employer must pay 100% of salary during non-compete period ("garden leave" model). Real cost on employer for using it.

**AI displacement + workforce transition:**
- **Apprenticeship expansion in trades** — federal funding for paid apprenticeship programs in plumbing, electrical, HVAC, welding, nursing, lineman, etc. AI-resistant jobs that built America. Counter to "everyone needs college" narrative.
- **Universal HSA-style Worker Account** — every worker has one tax-advantaged account funded by payroll. Holds: emergency fund + skills training + portable benefits + retirement. Single account follows worker across jobs. Replaces patchwork of 401(k) / health / UI / training.
- Cross-link: this is the same architecture as Universal HSA (Healthcare), Housing Savings Account (Housing), and sovereign wealth fund-backed Social Security. Theme: Americans own their financial future.

**Dem position:** $15–22 federal min wage flat, PRO Act (expand union org), gig workers = employees.
**Rep position:** State-level min wage, national right-to-work, free contractors, deregulate.
**Citizens Party:** Tiered min wage by age + hours + region + CPI + Independent Worker classification + portable benefits + non-compete ban under $150K + paid garden leave above + apprenticeship expansion + Universal Worker Account.

## 4. Cross-Cutting Threads

These themes connect sub-issues. The pillar page highlights them as the narrative of Economics:

1. **"Americans own their financial future."**
   - Sovereign wealth fund (SS trust fund) — debt sub-issue
   - Universal HSA from birth — healthcare sub-issue
   - Housing Savings Account — housing sub-issue
   - Universal Worker Account — wages sub-issue
   - One architecture (tax-advantaged, portable, federally-seeded for low-income) applied to four life needs.

2. **12-month federal permitting cap.**
   - Energy projects — inflation sub-issue
   - Strategic manufacturing reshoring — trade sub-issue
   - Same rule. Applied twice. Could extend to Housing (currently 6-month local cap via YIMBY incentive).

3. **Drug price negotiation, stacked.**
   - Medicare leverage — debt sub-issue (entitlement reform)
   - International reference pricing for whole market — healthcare sub-issue
   - Stacks: Medicare gets the best rate, market gets the median peer-nation rate.

4. **Pentagon accountability + defense reshore.**
   - 5% audit-failure cut + leadership swap — debt sub-issue
   - Reshored defense manufacturing + Buy American 100% — trade sub-issue
   - Same theme (accountable, domestic, smart military spending), two enforcement mechanisms.

5. **Captivity = Regulation, Choice = Market.**
   - PBM divestiture — healthcare sub-issue
   - App Store 5% cap OR sideloading — Big Tech sub-issue
   - Site-neutral payments — healthcare sub-issue (hospital captive markets)
   - Disney/stadium/Ticketmaster markup cap — Big Tech sub-issue (cross-industry)
   - Unifying principle across pillar — and likely across other pillars.

6. **Sunset clauses + performance audits.**
   - 10-year sunset on every law + agency — debt sub-issue
   - Performance audits → cut funding when missing 3 yrs — debt sub-issue
   - Reshoring credit sunset at 10 years — trade sub-issue
   - 5% Medicare cut for non-compliance with transparency rule — healthcare sub-issue
   - Theme: every policy has an expiration date and a measurable outcome.

7. **No corporate donors. No structural breakups for chosen relationships.**
   - Citizens Party doesn't take corporate money, so we don't reward corporate consolidation OR punish it for ideological reasons. We punish it when consumers are captive (Captivity principle). We leave it alone when consumers are free.
